# g-factor

The g-factor is a statistical construct, not a thing in the head. It names the shared variance across many cognitive tests when administered to the same people.

When researchers factor-analyse scores on a battery of cognitive tests, the first factor explains a large share of the variance. That first factor is the g-factor. Fluid reasoning is one of the strongest contributors to it; verbal reasoning is another; visuospatial reasoning is another.

IQ-ME measures one narrow surface of fluid reasoning. A score on IQ-ME correlates with broader g-factor measures because of that shared variance. The instrument does not measure g directly. A multi-domain IQ battery comes closer; even that approach yields an estimate of g, not g itself.
