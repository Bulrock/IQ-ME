# IQ-ME licensing

IQ-ME's source and methodology are released under two licenses. The split reflects the project's intent. The app source is free to fork and modify. The methodology content is shared but not relicensable as a credential.

The application source code uses the MIT license. A reader can clone the repository, modify any source file, build a derivative, and ship it under their own brand. The MIT license is the most permissive of the standard open-source licenses. The project chose it to make replication and audit easy.

The methodology corpus content uses Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike 4.0 International. The short form is CC-BY-NC-SA-4.0. A reader can quote the methodology in their own work with attribution. A reader can build a derivative methodology that adapts the original. A reader cannot use the methodology in a commercial product without separate terms.

The split exists for a reason. The methodology corpus is the part of the project that asserts what the screener measures. If a commercial actor could relicense the methodology, they could attach IQ-ME's claims to a different instrument. The public record would then fail to tell the two apart. The NC clause prevents that.

The two licenses are documented in `LICENSES.md` at the repository root. The CITATION.cff file declares the project metadata in a machine-readable form. Citation managers read CFF directly. The provenance section also includes the ICAR-license page that documents the underlying item-pool license.

A contributor to IQ-ME accepts the licensing as a condition of contribution. The CONTRIBUTING.md document at the repository root describes the contribution process. It also covers the developer-certificate-of-origin attestation that contributors make.
