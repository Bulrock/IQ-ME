# Methodology comparison — accuracy and popularity

IQ-ME now offers more than one test. This page compares them. The goal is to help you choose before you start. The comparison is grounded in the methodology research that gated this work.

Two methodologies ship today. Both come from the ICAR public item pool. Both score through the same engine. Both carry the same honest framing.

## What each test measures

Geometric matrix reasoning shows a grid of shapes with one cell missing. You pick the shape that completes the pattern. It is a classic marker of fluid reasoning. It is the most recognized nonverbal test format. The Raven's family of tests made it well known.

Letter and number series shows a short sequence with one term missing. You find the rule and pick the missing term. It is a marker of inductive reasoning, which sits close to fluid reasoning. The format is common in aptitude tests. It is language-neutral, since it uses digits and letters.

## Accuracy

Both tests measure reasoning that loads on general ability. Matrix reasoning is a long-studied marker of fluid intelligence. Series tasks are a long-studied marker of inductive reasoning. A short browser screen of either is a screen, not a precise measurement. The result page shows a range, not a single number, for this reason.

We do not publish a single accuracy figure here. A figure without its study and sample would mislead. The honest claim is qualitative: both formats are established reasoning markers, and a short screen gives a rough estimate with a stated range.

## Popularity

Matrix reasoning is the more widely recognized format. Most people have seen a "complete the pattern" item. Series items are common in school and job aptitude tests, so they are familiar too, if a little less iconic.

## Openness

Both tests use ICAR items. ICAR is one of the few public, license-free pools for cognitive research. This is why IQ-ME can offer them in an open, free, browser-based screen. Better-known clinical batteries, such as the Wechsler scales, are proprietary. They cannot be redistributed in a tool like this, so IQ-ME does not implement them.

## What this comparison is not

This is a screen, not a diagnosis. Neither test is a clinical assessment or a credential. The comparison helps you pick a format that suits you. It does not rank you against other people in any official way.
