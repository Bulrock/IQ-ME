# Story 14.9: Ink-economical Aurora print/PDF result document

## Story

As a **test-taker who exports or prints my result for my own records**,
I want **the printed/PDF page to be a deliberate ink-economical document rather than a screenshot of the glass UI**,
so that **I get a clear, high-contrast, honestly-framed summary that reads correctly on white paper in any of the three locales**.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. **No Aurora glass bleeds into the printed document (PR-26).** After the Story 14.2 Aurora token revaluation landed a deep-navy spatial backdrop (`body { background-image: radial-gradient(... --backdrop-aurora-1/2 ...) }` in `base.css`) and retuned glass fills consumed by the screen result via `score-panel.css` (`--surface-glass-strong` + `backdrop-filter: blur(var(--surface-glass-blur))` + the lit edge), the printed/exported document carries NONE of it: the `@media print` block keeps forcing `color-scheme: light` with `#ffffff` background and `#111111` ink, and the printed `.result-scene`/`.score-panel` surfaces render with `box-shadow: none`, `border: none`, a `42rem` reading measure, and — added defensively in this story — an explicit `background-image: none` so the deep-navy aurora gradient cannot survive into print even if the white-bg `background:` shorthand reset is later refactored to a longhand. The screen glass blur is deliberately NOT re-declared in the print block: on the forced-white print surface `backdrop-filter: blur()` composites over white (a visual no-op), and the print block stays entirely free of any `backdrop-filter` artifact — the frozen Story 13-5 PR-17 invariant (`tests/scaffold/13-5-print-result.test.mjs` asserts the print block contains NO `backdrop-filter`), which this story honors and reinforces rather than overriding. No `--surface-glass*` translucency role and no `--backdrop-*` deep-navy role applies in the printed block. The zero-third-party / no-inline-`<style>` invariant (NFR6/NFR7) holds: print restyling stays in `print.css` (not in the `css-components-lines` budget domain `src/css/components/**`), with no new runtime dependency, and the byte-stable double-build diff stays clean (NFR21).

2. **Documented design decision: scientific-neutral, no accent (PR-26).** A code-comment decision block is added at the head of the `@media print` section in `print.css` stating and justifying the chosen direction: print is **scientific-neutral, ink-economical, grayscale-printer-fidelity — no Aurora color token bleeds into print, and no neutral hairline accent is introduced beyond the existing hairline rules** (the decision is: no accent at all). The two justifications are recorded: (1) ink economy — a colour wash/tinted accent wastes toner for a self-records document; (2) grayscale-printer fidelity — a blue-violet accent degrades unpredictably and can lower contrast on a greyscale printer, whereas a pure dark-ink ramp is exact on any device. The Aurora *identity* is expressed only through type, rule, and spacing. The printed masthead title, date, triplet, difficulty sentence, and footer use ONLY the dark-ink ramp already defined (`#111111`/`#333333`/`#555555`/`#777777`) plus the `#cccccc` hairline — no Aurora color hex bleeds in.

3. **Co-equal triplet stays co-equal (FR18 / PR-26).** The Percentile / IQ-scale / Range triplet stays visually co-equal in print: `.score-panel__percentile`, `.score-panel__anchor`, and `.score-panel__band` print at uniform `#111111` weight with the preserved triplet flex layout (`gap`/`margin-block` tuned, never collapsed) — no member is emphasized over another, and the `.score-panel__metric-label` prints at the quiet `#555555` label ink. No triplet typography is touched.

4. **Honest-framing invariant preserved — caveat never suppressed (PR-13 / PR-26).** The disclaimer renders on screen as a collapsed native `<details class="disclaimer score-panel__explainer">` and the difficulty sentence is reveal-gated; in print the full not-a-certificate / not-a-clinical-assessment disclaimer is forced open and printed in entirety (`.disclaimer` + `.disclaimer__summary` + body shown, `::marker`/`::-webkit-details-marker` hidden) and `.score-panel__difficulty-sentence` is forced `display: block`, so no ink-saving or reveal-gating ever suppresses the caveat. The printed page is explicitly NOT a credential, with the disclaimer and `.score-panel__caveat` (PR-13) intact alongside the co-equal triplet.

5. **Interactive chrome does not leak; print-only masthead/footer appear (PR-26).** `.chrome-header`, `.chrome-footer`, `.result-print-btn`, `.score-panel__save-button`, `.score-panel__retest-note`, `.rs-show`, `.rs-not`, and `.tail-scene` are all `display: none` in print, while the print-only masthead `.result-print-only` and footer `.result-print-footer` (hidden on screen, emitted by `PRINT_HEAD`/`PRINT_FOOTER` in `result.js`) become visible. The frozen Epic 11/13 DOM contracts are preserved: no markup class names are renamed, the score-panel co-equal triplet structure is untouched, and `result.js` is byte-identical (restyle-only; no JS/markup edit). The footer remains the locale-agnostic structural identity line `IQ-ME · /methodology/${CV}/` carrying no translatable prose, so it introduces no NFR27 translation-parity cascade — no `sourceHashEN` re-bump and no i18n string touched.

6. **RU/PL print without clipping + Node guard runs locally; rendered verification deferred to 14.11.** The document is constrained to a wrapping `42rem` measure (never a fixed width) so longer RU/PL `printTitle`/`caveat`/`resultExplainer`/`difficultySentenceTemplate`/`bandTemplate` strings wrap rather than clip, and `break-inside: avoid` keeps `.score-panel__triplet`, `.disclaimer`, and `.result-print-footer` from orphaning across page breaks. A node guard (`tests/scaffold/14-9-print-document.test.mjs`) RUNS in `make test` and asserts: (a) `color-scheme: light` + `#ffffff` bg + `#111111` ink; (b) the printed surfaces' explicit `backdrop-filter: none` + `-webkit-backdrop-filter: none` + `background-image: none` + white fill + `box-shadow: none` + `border: none` + `42rem` measure; (c) no `--surface-glass*`/`--backdrop-*` role and no `backdrop-filter: blur` survives in the print block; (d) chrome/buttons/reveal/tail `display: none` + the print masthead/footer shown; (e) the disclaimer force-open (details + summary shown, marker hidden) + difficulty `display: block`; (f) the co-equal triplet uniform `#111111` + preserved layout; (g) `break-inside: avoid` on triplet/disclaimer/footer; (h) the documented decision comment block at the head of the print section; (i) only the neutral dark-ink/hairline ramp hexes survive; (j) the frozen `result.js` print-only DOM contract. The RENDERED print/PDF leg (the document actually printing high-contrast ink on white with no glass, in EN/RU/PL) is the dormant Playwright print leg whose baselines Story 14.11 commits on `ubuntu-latest` (the authoring host is darwin). `print.css` is NOT in the `css-components-lines` budget domain (`src/css/components/**`), so `css-components-lines` stays unchanged at 2336.

## Dev Notes

### Implementation strategy
- **The print path was already largely correct.** The `@media print` block (Epic 13 PR-17, Story 13-5) already forced `color-scheme: light`, `#ffffff` bg, `#111111` ink, hid the interactive chrome, showed the print-only masthead/footer, forced the disclaimer open + the difficulty sentence to `display: block`, printed the co-equal triplet at a uniform `#111111`, set the `42rem` reading measure, and applied `break-inside: avoid`. This story is NOT a rewrite — it (a) records the documented design decision per AC clause 2 and (b) closes the two defensive Aurora-bleed gaps the Story 14.2 token revaluation exposed.
- **Audit — two candidate gaps; only one is safe to close.** (1) The printed `.result-scene, .score-panel` rule reset `background`, `box-shadow`, and `border` but never reset `backdrop-filter` — so the screen glass blur filter technically survives into print. On the forced-white print background this is a visual no-op (it blurs white). The frozen Story 13-5 guard (`tests/scaffold/13-5-print-result.test.mjs`, lines 36-41) explicitly asserts the print block contains NO `backdrop-filter` literal at all — its authors chose absence over `backdrop-filter: none` precisely because the white-bg reset makes the blur moot. Adding `backdrop-filter: none` would trip that frozen guard with a NEW failure, so this story deliberately does NOT add it, honoring the existing invariant. (2) The deep-navy aurora `background-image` on `body` is currently reset only IMPLICITLY by the `background: #ffffff !important` shorthand (a shorthand resets all background sub-properties to initial); to make the intent legible and robust against a future refactor of that shorthand to a longhand, this story adds an explicit `background-image: none !important` on the printed surfaces (no frozen guard forbids it). The added declaration is purely additive, deterministic CSS — byte-stable (NFR21).
- **Scientific-neutral, no accent.** AC clause 2 required a documented choice between a simplified Aurora accent ink and a scientific-neutral document. The decision is scientific-neutral with NO accent: a colour accent wastes ink and degrades unpredictably on greyscale printers, and the design direction §3.3 already pins "no aurora, no blur, no glow, no grid, no dark backdrop in print" with the identity expressed via type/rule/spacing. The decision block records this, the two justifications, and the allowed dark-ink ramp.
- **No JS / markup / i18n / corpus touched.** `result.js` (`PRINT_HEAD`/`PRINT_FOOTER`/`DISCLAIMER` emitters), the i18n strings, and the methodology corpus are byte-identical. The print footer is a locale-agnostic structural identity line (`IQ-ME · /methodology/${CV}/`) with no translatable prose, so there is no NFR27 PL/RU cascade and no `sourceHashEN` re-bump. The frozen Epic 11/13 DOM contracts and the co-equal triplet structure are preserved.
- **Budget.** `print.css` lives at `src/css/print.css`, NOT under `src/css/components/**`, so it does not count toward the `css-components-lines` budget — that domain stays at 2336. The change is a print.css comment block + three defensive declarations.
