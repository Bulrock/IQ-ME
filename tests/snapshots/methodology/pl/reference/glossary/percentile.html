A percentile rank tells you what share of the reference population scores at or below a given score.

A percentile of 75 means about three quarters of the reference population scored at or below that level. A percentile of 50 is the population median. Percentiles run from 1 to 99 in IQ-ME; we round and clip at the ends to avoid implying false precision near the tails where the data thins out.

The reference population is the norming sample used to calibrate the percentile-to-IQ-scale transform. The sample skews young, online, and educated. A percentile from IQ-ME is a position within that population, not a global standing.

The percentile anchor sits next to the IQ-scale anchor and the uncertainty band on the result page. The three describe the same score in three different ways.
