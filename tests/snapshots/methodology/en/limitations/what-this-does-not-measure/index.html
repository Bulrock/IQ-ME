What this instrument does not measure

IQ-ME is a narrow screener of one surface of one cognitive ability. This page exists to make the boundaries of that claim plain. The page is protected (FR36): it cannot be shortened or removed without a CHANGELOG entry and a reviewer-of-record sign-off. The point is not procedural. The point is that under-claiming is the discipline that lets the rest of the project breathe.

The instrument does not measure intelligence as a whole-person property. It does not measure verbal reasoning, working memory in isolation, processing speed, attention regulation under load, learned skill, social judgment, persistence, or any clinical condition. Many things observers call intelligence are not detectable by 16 matrix items in a single sitting.

The instrument does not measure performance under time pressure. There is no time limit per item. There is no overall session timer that affects the score. A test-taker who pauses for a glass of water is not penalised. A test-taker who finishes faster is not credited. The score reflects pattern recognition under self-paced conditions only.

The instrument does not measure stability of ability over time. A score on a session today is a snapshot. The same person on a different day, with different sleep, different distractions, different mood, would produce a slightly different point estimate. The uncertainty band quantifies a single session's noise; it does not quantify between-session drift. The retest-effects page in the limitations section addresses what repeated taking does to the score.

The instrument does not measure ability across cultural-linguistic contexts equivalently. The norming sample skews young, online, English-speaking, and educated. The construct of fluid reasoning is widely studied as comparatively culture-fair relative to verbal tests, but the calibration data for the percentile-to-IQ-scale conversion comes from this specific population. A reader in a different population should treat the IQ-scale anchor as approximate, not as a cross-cultural standard.

The instrument does not diagnose. It does not screen for learning disabilities, cognitive decline, attention deficit, autism spectrum traits, or any other condition. A reader concerned about any of these surfaces should speak with a qualified clinician. The instrument is not a substitute for that conversation.

The instrument does not produce a clinical record. The session does not transmit, store on a server, or log a result to a database. The result page lives in the browser tab. Closing the tab discards it. A test-taker who chooses to save the result through the explicit opt-in save flow stores it locally only. There is no provider-side history that an employer, an insurer, or a clinician can subpoena.

The instrument does not measure your worth. A high score is one signal from one short test taken once. A low score is one signal from one short test taken once. People build lives on persistence, judgment, repair, repair-of-repair, learning, kindness, and care for what matters; none of those are visible to 16 matrix items. The IQ-scale number is small; the life is large.

If any of the boundaries above are unclear after reading this page, the limitations section of the methodology corpus expands on each in dedicated pages. The retest-effects page covers what taking the test repeatedly means for the score. The cultural-variance page covers the norming-sample skew in more detail. The anti-leakage page covers what the project does to keep the item set out of practice corpora that would inflate scores for repeat takers. The result page itself carries a caveat that summarises the most load-bearing of these boundaries.

The discipline of saying what this instrument does not measure is the same discipline that lets it be useful for what it does measure. A screener of fluid reasoning is a useful surface for some readers some of the time. It is not a measurement of a person.
