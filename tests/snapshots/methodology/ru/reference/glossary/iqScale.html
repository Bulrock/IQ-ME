The IQ-scale is the convention that anchors a score on a familiar range. It transforms a latent-ability estimate onto a scale where the population mean is 100 and the standard deviation is 15.

The formula in IQ-ME is IQ-scale = round(100 + 15 × theta), where theta is the ability estimate produced by the scoring model from a session's responses.

The IQ-scale number is a transform of one underlying estimate. The transform itself adds no information; it just expresses the same estimate on a scale most readers have seen before in popular and clinical writing about cognitive ability. The percentile anchor and the uncertainty band are the other two surfaces of the same score.

A test-taker who treats the IQ-scale number as a population standing of their whole person is over-claiming the score. The score is one narrow signal of one narrow ability.
