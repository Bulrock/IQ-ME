The glossary defines the load-bearing terms the IQ-ME methodology uses. Each entry links to its own short page. Pages are kept narrow on purpose: a glossary is not a textbook.

The entries cover the cognitive-ability terms the result page links to and the psychometric terms the scoring pages link to. New terms are added when a methodology page introduces one.

The list:

  • The fluid-reasoning g-factor entry: what the g-factor is and is not as a statistical construct.
  • The IQ-scale entry: what the mean-100, standard-deviation-15 transform represents.
  • The percentile entry: how to read a percentile rank as a population position.
  • The standard error of measurement entry: what the SEM is and how it relates to a session's noise.
  • The uncertainty entry: how the displayed band combines test noise and norming-sample noise.

Each entry is one short page. None are textbook chapters. A reader who needs a deeper account should consult the full methodology corpus or the reference list.
