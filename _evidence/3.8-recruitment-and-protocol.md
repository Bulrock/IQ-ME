# Story 3.8 hallway-test — recruitment and session protocol

This document is the maintainer's playbook for running the Story 3.8 aha-click hallway test. The goal: 3-5 outside-team English-speaking participants complete the Epic-3 vertical slice on a local build, answer four interview questions, and the aggregate verdict gates Epic 4 start.

The test is NOT a polished UX study. It is an informal hallway test in the original sense — quick, low-friction, written up as qualitative evidence in `_evidence/3.8-hallway-test-<handle>.md` per participant.

## Recruitment sources

Per the epic-spec, recruitment draws from sources OUTSIDE the project team:

- Friends or family without a psychometric background.
- Online communities — for example a low-key DM in `r/cognitiveTesting` or a friendly post on Mastodon. Use discretion; do not blast threads.
- An HN-comment-equivalent recruitment, if the maintainer is comfortable.

The build is **visibly pre-release**. There is no marketing copy, no signup, no analytics. Recruitment language is:

> I'm building something. Would you try this self-paced assessment and tell me what you think?

That is the pitch. Do not over-sell it. Do not promise the build is finished. Do not call it a "study".

## Recruitment cold-message template

A 3-sentence DM the maintainer can paste, adapted per recipient:

> Hi <name>, I'm building a small non-commercial fluid-reasoning screener and I'd love a 20-minute hallway test from someone who isn't on the project. You'd take a 16-item test, see your result, click through to the methodology, and then I'd ask you four short questions about what you saw. The build is intentionally pre-release; you'd be helping me find what's confusing before I commit to writing 30 more methodology pages.

If the recipient asks for compensation, decline politely; the project is non-commercial and the maintainer is not running paid testing. If the recipient asks for what they get out of it, say: an interesting brief experience + the maintainer's genuine gratitude + a credit in the aggregate write-up if they consent to a handle.

## The four interview questions (verbatim, from epic-spec)

Ask these AFTER the participant has clicked through to a methodology page and read it. Ask them in this order, open-endedly. Do NOT lead the participant toward any answer.

1. What did your score mean?
2. What did you learn by clicking through to methodology?
3. Would you trust this site to a friend?
4. Did anything feel off?

Capture each answer verbatim in `_evidence/3.8-hallway-test-<handle>.md`. Verbatim quotes are the evidence; the maintainer's coded summary is the aggregation surface.

## Consent and redaction

Before the session, ask the participant:

> I'd like to take notes during this and write them up in a public-facing aggregate summary. Three options:
> 1. I'd publish your handle if you want credit.
> 2. I'd publish a pseudonym (e.g. "P3") and redact identifying details.
> 3. You'd prefer the notes are maintainer-eyes-only.
> Which would you prefer?

Default to pseudonym if the participant is unsure. The **aggregate summary** is always publishable; **individual session files** are gated on participant choice. Maintainer-eyes-only files MAY be gitignored — that is a maintainer decision at write-time.

## Session protocol

Target 20 minutes; hard ceiling 30 minutes.

1. **Setup (3-5 min)**: walk the participant through cloning the repo + running `make build-methodology && make dev` + opening `http://127.0.0.1:4173/`. If they get stuck, screen-share. Record setup observations in the evidence file — setup friction IS data.
2. **Landing → consent (2 min)**: let them read the landing page and consent disclosure on their own. Do not narrate.
3. **16-item session (8-12 min)**: let them complete the matrix items at their own pace. Do not coach. The pre-reveal beat ("Your result is ready / Show me") is part of the experience; let them encounter it.
4. **Reveal + score panel (1-2 min)**: they click "Show me" and see the score panel with the triplet (percentile / IQ-scale / uncertainty band). Watch for what they click on first. Watch for surprised laughter, sighs, or "huh?".
5. **Methodology click (1-2 min)**: ideally they click a number voluntarily. If they don't click in 60 seconds, gently prompt: "is there anything you'd like to know more about?". A prompted click is still data; record it as prompted.
6. **The four questions (4-6 min)**: ask in order. Capture verbatim. Do not lead.

## Maintainer's go/no-go protocol

Per the epic-spec:

- **GO** if ≥3 of N participants comprehend the score AND ≥3 of N click methodology voluntarily.
- **GO-WITH-BRIDGE** if any participant reports the click felt confusing OR the page was incomprehensible OR the handoff felt jarring. The bridge candidate(s) are documented in the aggregate summary and picked up by `epic-3-retrospective` as Bridge Plan items per `docs/corpus-build-conventions.md` retro flow.
- **NO-GO** only if ≥2 of N report the score-to-methodology handoff is fundamentally broken (e.g., "I had no idea what the page was about" + "the click made me distrust the whole site"). NO-GO blocks Epic 4 start and triggers a story 3.8b: re-design effort.

If N=3 and the maintainer is uncertain, recruit 1-2 more participants to disambiguate. If N=5 and the maintainer is still uncertain, the hypothesis itself may be unfalsifiable as currently stated; document this in the aggregate summary as an "open hypothesis" rather than forcing a verdict.

## Known interim limitations to flag pre-session

So participants don't report "broken site" for things the maintainer already knows:

- The methodology pages are stubs. Their bodies are raw markdown wrapped in `<pre>` (Epic 4 lands the styled corpus).
- Only EN locale is available (Epic 7 lands RU/PL).
- The 16 matrix items are placeholders, not the full ICAR-MR pool (Epic 9a finalizes the licensed item pool).

A participant who reports "the methodology page looked ugly" SHOULD have that observation recorded — it's qualitative evidence — but the maintainer codes it as "expected interim" rather than "tonal-handoff issue". A participant who reports "the methodology page looked ugly AND I didn't understand what it was saying" — the second clause IS a tonal-handoff issue and the maintainer codes it as such.

## Where evidence lives

- Per-participant: `_evidence/3.8-hallway-test-<handle-or-pseudonym>.md`. Use the template at `_evidence/3.8-hallway-test-template.md`.
- Aggregate: `_evidence/3.8-hallway-test-summary.md` (DROP the `-template` suffix when filled in). Use the template at `_evidence/3.8-hallway-test-summary-template.md`.

The aggregate summary is the document Epic 4's first PR description cites as evidence the hypothesis is provisionally validated.
